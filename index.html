The Direction

One Voice, Two Registers

Concept 1's timeless sophistication anchors the brand across menus, signage, and the physical experience. Concept 3's contemporary edge sharpens the digital presence — where Gen Z and millennial "coastal tastemakers" will discover Zunzibar first.

80%
Refined Coastal Classic
  • Maritime Navy + Coastal Sand + Coastal Rattan foundation
  • Cormorant Garamond as primary display typeface
  • Botanical palm tree illustration in logo
  • Embossed menus, warm-toned collateral, linen textures
  • Timeless, confident, "lived-in luxury" tone
  • Drives: physical space, menus, signage, merch, print
20%
Modern Coastal
  • Coral Sunset as digital CTA and action color
  • DM Sans for UI navigation, buttons, and forms
  • Charcoal for enhanced digital readability
  • Clean, bold icon treatments for social avatars
  • Contemporary, design-forward energy for screens
  • Drives: website, social media, email, digital ads

Typography

Three Fonts, Clear Roles

Cormorant Garamond carries the brand's soul. Outfit handles readability across body copy. DM Sans brings Concept 3's contemporary clarity to digital interfaces.

Display / Headlines
Cormorant Garamond

Use: Logo wordmark, headlines, menu headers, section titles, hero text

Weights: Light (300), Regular (400), Medium (500), Semibold (600)

Source: Concept 1 — available on Google Fonts + Adobe Fonts

Body / Content
Outfit — clean, geometric, modern readability with warmth

Use: Paragraphs, descriptions, menu item copy, about sections

Weights: Light (200), Regular (300–400), Medium (500)

Source: Brand system — available on Google Fonts

UI / Digital Interface
DM Sans — buttons, navigation, forms, captions, metadata

Use: Website nav, CTAs, form labels, social overlays, email headers

Weights: Regular (400), Medium (500), Semibold (600), Bold (700)

Source: Concept 3 influence — available on Google Fonts

Application Rules

When to Use What

✦ Coral Sunset — Do

  • Website CTAs (Reserve, Order, View Menu)
  • Social media story highlights and swipe-up links
  • Email buttons and action prompts
  • Digital ads — primary action button
  • Instagram grid accent overlays
  • Menu category labels (digital menus only)

✦ Coral Sunset — Don't

  • Physical menu headers or printed signage
  • Logo lockup backgrounds
  • Large background fills on the website
  • Replace Coastal Rattan on print materials
  • Use as body text color
  • Pair directly with Coastal Rattan (visual clash)

✦ DM Sans — Do

  • Website navigation and button text
  • Form labels and input fields
  • Social media overlay text and captions
  • Category labels and metadata (H3+ level)
  • Digital advertising copy
  • Email pre-headers and utility text

✦ DM Sans — Don't

  • Replace Cormorant Garamond in headlines
  • Use for printed menu item names
  • Use for the primary logo wordmark
  • Replace Outfit for body paragraphs
  • Use in light weights below 400 (readability)
  • Set larger than 16pt — it's a utility font here
